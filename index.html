Mac Miller

(REMember Music)

Musician, Artist (1992-2018)

10 Things You Didn't Know About Mac Miller:

  • Mac Miller was the stage name of Malcolm James McCormick, originally from Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.
  • Malcolm was an incredibly talented musician from a very young age, he taught himself how to play piano, guitar, drums and bass all by the time he was six years old.
  • He released his first mixtape at the age of fifteen under the name Easy (EZ) Mac in 2007. Three years later, at the 2010 Pittsburgh Hip Hop Awards he would go on to win both the 21 & Under of the Year and Best Hip Hop Video awards.
  • In 2013 he founded the record label trademark/imprint of REMember Music which primarily focused on building up Pittsburgh artists.
  • Mac Miller's music has an amazing psychedelic sound to it which really started to develop after his second studio album 'Watching Movies with the Sound Off' was released.
  • Mac Miller served as a record producer for various artists, including himself, under the pseudonym Larry Fisherman.
  • Mac Miller has named some of his biggest influencers as the Lauryn Hill, Beastie Boys, Outkast, A Tribe Called Quest and Wiz Khalifa.
  • Mac Miller has a tendency to be confused with Mack Miller, an American cross-country skier who competed in the Winter Olypics.
  • Despite his hard work ethic and amazing musical talents he stuggled with substance abuse and depression and he made multiple references to this in his music before dying from an accidental drug overdose at the age of 26.
  • After passing away posthumous music was released which included the album, 'Circles' which he had been working on before death. One of the songs on that album, 'Good News', became his highest charting song.
Today is full of regret, find forever in tomorrow.
